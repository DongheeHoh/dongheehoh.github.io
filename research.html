Research

My research focuses on photosynthetic regulation under real-world fluctuation toward resilient performance and practical impact. This page highlights only broad themes; please contact me directly for more details.

-->

My research aims to understand how genetic diversity and environmental fluctuations interact to shape photosynthetic resilience, with the goal of translating this understanding into improved crop performance and climate-resilient productivity.

To move toward this goal, my research tackles several key questions that remain open between fundamental mechanisms and real-world applications:

Mechanistic layer

Two complementary strategies using natural genetic diversity and field-like dynamic environments reveal hidden regulatory mechanisms that are not captured in static, mutant-based studies.

JXB 2025 — LIgHT (DOI) PCE 2024 — Redox (DOI) PCE 2022 — Lipid/Chilling (DOI)

Scaling layer

Connecting second-scale regulation to season-long carbon gain and yield across temporal (seconds→seasons) and spatial (chloroplast→canopy) scales, including source–sink coordination.

For details or collaboration inquiries, please contact me directly.

Translational layer

Mechanism-informed prediction and climate-resilient improvement focused on stable performance under fluctuation.

For details or collaboration inquiries, please contact me directly.

Broad research questions

  • Mechanistic How do plants maintain energy balance and resilience under fluctuating environments (not static laboratory conditions)?
  • Mechanistic How does natural genetic diversity shape the regulatory networks underlying photosynthetic responses (not just single mutants)?
  • Scaling How do rapid photosynthetic regulations scale over time and space — from chloroplast mechanisms to canopy productivity — to drive season-long carbon gain and yield?
  • Translational How can mechanistic understanding guide predictive modeling and climate-resilient crop improvement?
