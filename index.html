Photosynthesis Natural Genetic Variation Spectroscopy

From mechanisms to resilient plant performance

I study how dynamic environments and natural genetic diversity shape photosynthetic regulation, and how we can translate that understanding into predictive models and practical impact.

Big questions

  • What limits plants’ ability to use or dissipate light energy?
  • How do dynamic environments expose hidden limitations?
  • How does genetic variation modulate these responses?

Approach

By integrating a diverse set of techniques—including biochemistry, biophysics, kinetic and crop modeling, quantitative genetics, and plant physiology—I connect molecular regulation with whole-plant performance. My approach combines spectroscopy-guided measurements with computational modeling and data analysis to bridge mechanism and prediction.

Current focus

  • 🧬 How does genetic variation shape photosynthetic responses?
  • 🌍 How do dynamic environments reveal hidden limitations?
  • 🌱 How can we better understand complex G × E interactions?

Two approaches to mechanistic studies

🧬 Genetic diversity uncovers hidden regulatory mechanisms revealed through natural variation.

🌿 Dynamic environments challenge plants to expose limits unseen under stable conditions.

Mechanistic insight sparks understanding — prediction and translation carry it forward.
