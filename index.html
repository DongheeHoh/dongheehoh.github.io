Photosynthesis Natural Genetic Variation Spectroscopy

From mechanisms to resilient plant performance

I study how dynamic environments and natural genetic diversity shape photosynthetic regulation, and how we can translate that understanding into predictive models and practical impact.

Big questions

  • What limits plants’ ability to use or dissipate light energy?
  • How do dynamic environments expose hidden limitations?
  • How does genetic variation modulate these responses?

Approach

Spectroscopy-guided experiments, quantitative genetics, and modeling. I integrate tools like pulse-amplitude modulation, ECS, and imaging with data analysis in Python/R.

Current focus

Photoprotection and redox regulation under fluctuating light and temperature; connecting leaf dynamics to canopy productivity without sharing unpublished proposal content.

Two complementary lenses

Genetic diversity reveals mechanisms hiding in plain sight.

Dynamic environments stress systems to expose regulation that static assays miss.

Mechanistic insight is the spark; prediction and translation carry it forward.
